History

Perhaps more than a thousand years ago, in China, the bonsai art form was developed. By the year 700 AD, the Chinese had developed the "pun-sai" art, which involved using unique methods to cultivate and care for tiny trees in containers.

Pun-tsai was originally only practiced by the affluent of society, but as opulent gifts, bonsai later spread throughout China. The Japanese pronunciation of the word "bonsai" is derived from the Chinese word penzai. All small trees in pots or containers are referred to as bonsai in English.

At least 1200 years ago, the first bonsai trees are thought to have been transported from China to Japan. A well-known Noh theater production was based on a folktale from the late 1300s about a destitute samurai who gave his last three dwarf potted trees to a wandering monk to keep him warm one chilly winter night.

A show for traditional pine miniature potted trees started to be held yearly in the capital city of Kyoto by the late 18th century. The economic impact of bonsai led Takamatsu to start cultivating dwarf pine fields as a major source of income.

A gathering of Chinese art experts near the city of Osaka took place around the year 1800 to discuss contemporary trends in miniature trees. Their tiny trees were given the new name "Bonsai" to set them apart from the common hachi-no-ki that many people took care of.

Various sizes and styles were developed in the following centuries. Catalogs and books on trees, tools and pots have been published. A few early formal shows were held. Over time, bonsai began to spread around the world and underwent many developments and changes to create the bonsai we know today.

Tips on taking care of Bonsai.



Checking the soil daily and watering it when it appears to be dry. Watering on routine should be avoided.

Pruning should be done regularly to maintain the shape of your bonsai. Maintenance pruning includes snipping away branches, buds and leaves.

Large particles like volcanic rocks or stones can be mixed into the soil to improve drainage. Clay can be added to improve water retention and fertilizers should be only added after watering.

Bonsai must be repotted every two to five years and the outer layers of root mass must be trimmed away.
