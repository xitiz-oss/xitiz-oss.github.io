Minitsu's Origin

In the 90s, japan went through devastating economic turmoil and a recession. While the turmoil (so-called the Lost Decades) would end in the 2000s, many unemployed salary workers had to find different lines of work. During this period, a small group of political enthusiasts in Kyoto japan got together occasionally to share their love of rearing and taking care of small trees called bonsai, and Minitsu was created.

Bonsai, the traditional Japanese art of growing miniature trees in household ceramic pots were developed from the Chinese art of peijing, which utilises mini trees and sceneries to produce a real-life natural scene. But unlike peijing, bonsai, which translates to “tree planting” in Japanese, focuses solely on trees. A bonsai tree doesn't supply much purpose other than to appreciate its beauty and admire the care and effort of the grower. Similarly, to these small group of workermen of Kyoto, Bonsai became an art form, something to create, something to take care of, something beautiful to give back to the world, and from the sales of bonsai trees, some income to take home.

This not-so-exclusive club for the art of Bonsai, Minitsu soon became a common ground for art and botany lovers. The name derives from the Japanese word “Minitsurī” abbreviation, which translates to “Mini Tree”. As the popularity of this small clique grew, so did its fame.

Entering the 2000s, despite Japan’s struggling economy, Minitsu began to take shape into a reputed niche organisation for Bonsai lovers. Foreigners from all over the world would come to Kyoto to learn from the best Bonsai caretakers in all of Japan. With this, Minitsu also had a financial benefit from selling rare and exclusive mini trees. By 2005, Minitsu had become one of the most reputed Bonsai sellers in the world.

Now in 2023, Minitsu has become one of the biggest Bonsai sellers of the world.
